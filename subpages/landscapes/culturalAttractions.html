China Modern History Museum/Presidential Palace

China Modern History Museum, commonly known as the Nanjing Presidential Palace, is located at No. 292 Jinjiang Road, Nanjing, China. It is the site of the Han Dynasty in the Ming Dynasty, the General Office of the Two Rivers of the Qing Dynasty and the Heavenly Kingdom House of the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom. In 1912, it was the Provisional Presidential Palace of the Republic of China, 1927. From 1 year to 1937 and from 1946 to 1948, it was the office of the Republic of China Nationalist Government. From May 1948 to April 1949, it was the presidential palace of the Republic of China. After the founding of the People’s Republic of China, it became the Jiangsu Provincial People’s Government. Unit office office. In 1982, it became the national key cultural relic protection unit in the name of Taiping Heavenly Kingdom Tianwangfu. In March 2003, it was relocated to the Museum of Modern Chinese Historical Sites in Nanjing. In 2004, it was named the National AAAA-level tourist attraction.

Xiaoling Tomb of Ming Dynasty

The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum was the tomb of the founding emperor Zhu Yuanzhang and Queen Ma of the Ming Dynasty. Due to the Queen's "filial piety", the name was Xiaoling. The grand and magnificent Mausoleum of Ming Dynasty as the head of China's Ming Tombs represented the highest achievements of architecture and stone carving art in the early Ming Dynasty, affecting the shape of the imperial tombs of the Ming and Qing dynasties for more than 500 years.


The Memorial Hall of the Victims in Nanjing Massacre by Japanese Invaders

The Memorial Hall for the Nanjing Massacre by the Japanese Invaders was the site-specific history museum established by the Nanjing Municipal People’s Government to commemorate the victims of the Nanjing Massacre. The site of the museum is located at the Jiangdong Gate in the southwest of Nanjing City. It was a major site for the Japanese army massacre and a burial place for the victims. The memorial was opened in 1985. After two expansions in 1995 and 2005, it now covers an area of approximately 74,000 square meters, a building area of 25,000 square meters, and an exhibition area of 9,800 square meters, including exhibition ceremonies, memorial sites, and Peace Park. There are four areas, including the exchange of collections, among which the exhibition includes three parts: the exhibition of squares, the display of remains, and the display of historical materials. The museum has collected more than 10,000 historical materials and cultural relics and is a national museum.
